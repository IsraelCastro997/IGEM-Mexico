Agriculture For
Future Development

The fall armyworm (FAW, Spodoptera frugiperda) is the main pest of corn (Zea mays) in Mexico and the world. In response, farmers are forced to use pesticides, but the ones currently used in our country (pyrethroids, especially alpha-cypermethrin) are categorized as Highly Hazardous Pesticides (HHP) and most of them are forbidden in other countries. In Mexico, they are the second leading cause of death by poisoning. In humans, they can cause liver metabolism alterations and neuronal degeneration, and traces of these pesticides have even been found in human milk. (Jiménez-Venegas, et. al, 2008; DGSV-CNRF, 2020; Aznar-Alemany, et. al, 2020)

we need to rethink our agricultural industry

The issue is not only a health problem; the insecticides most commonly used to combat the FAW also have several harmful effects on ecosystems. They affect organisms such as algae, birds, fish, mammals, and frogs, and their use negatively impacts climate change since ecosystems are fundamental climate-regulating agents. (Biga & Blaustein, 2013; Baruah & Chaurasia, 2020; Kanyika-Mbewem et. al, 2020; Xie, et. al, 2022).

Furthermore, it is expected that fall armyworm potential worldwide distribution area will increase by up to 8.33% due to rising temperatures from climate change, as it prefers warmer temperatures. (Ramasamy, et. al, 2022). This implies greater use of pesticides, leading to more climate change, creating a vicious cycle.

What inspired us?

One of our members grew up in Cosalá, a rural community in Sinaloa, Mexico. He comes from a family of farmers who grow maize. Thus, he is very aware of the problems described above. In our discussions about what problem we wanted to address, his description of how farmers suffered the aftermath of pesticides in the form of sudden fainting stood out to us. Understanding how significant and close to our reality this problem was, and recognizing that we have a strategic point of view to address it, along with the fact that corn in Mexico is a key symbol of our culture, made us realize that we had to choose this project. That is why we presented a preliminary version in the iGEM Design-League 2022, and now we are here to continue the development of the project in the iGEM competition.

Get to know us

Agriculture matters to
the future of development

That’s why we present our novel biopesticide. It will be based on piperamides produced through a synthetic metabolic pathway derived from black pepper (Piper nigrum) and expressed in Saccharomyces cerevisiae. The objective is to combat the eggs and larvae of the FAW in a sustainable fashion. We want to test it in real maize crops in Mexico, involving the interaction of the community. This process requires the discovery and characterization of various enzymes, since the full route of the pipearmide synthesis is not completely known. (Tavares, et. al, 2011; Tang, et. al, 2024).
